# DrapixAI Go-To-Market Strategy (Pre-Launch)

## 1) Positioning
- Core promise: Increase ecommerce conversion confidence with AI virtual try-on that is fast to test and controlled to launch.
- Category: Virtual try-on infrastructure for fashion ecommerce.
- Current launch scope: Upper-body garments only (state this clearly in all messaging).
- Primary differentiator: Guided flow from garment upload to confirmed product mappings before live rollout.

## 2) Ideal Customer Profiles
- DTC fashion brands (50 to 500 SKUs), Shopify or custom storefronts.
- Marketplace-first fashion sellers expanding into owned ecommerce.
- Agencies managing multiple fashion storefronts.
- Enterprise fashion teams needing controlled pilot before wide rollout.

## 2.1) Two-Track Segmentation Rule
- Track A (current focus): Growth-stage web stores that need faster conversion experimentation.
- Track B (separate motion): Large/populated enterprise brands with longer buying cycles and custom procurement steps.
- Rule: Do not mix Track A and Track B in the same outreach batch or campaign assets.

## 3) Pre-Launch Offer
- 300 try-ons free trial over 12 days.
- Low-risk pilot with measurable outcomes in 7 to 14 days.
- Integration-first onboarding: upload garments, product discovery, suggested mapping, human confirmation, preview, then launch.

## 4) Messaging Pillars
- Revenue impact: More buyer confidence on product pages can improve add-to-cart and conversion.
- Speed to pilot: Trial can be set up quickly without full platform migration.
- Controlled rollout: Validate quality before exposing traffic.
- Commercial clarity: Starter/Growth plans for predictable monthly usage; enterprise path for custom volume.

## 5) Channel Strategy
- LinkedIn founder-led content:
  - 4 posts/week: before-after demos, onboarding walk-throughs, pilot outcomes, FAQ clips.
- Instagram + Reels:
  - 3 short videos/week focused on visual quality and shopper journey.
- YouTube Shorts:
  - Repurpose best-performing reels with stronger SEO titles.
- Cold email to fashion ecommerce brands:
  - 4-touch sequence over 10 days with clear CTA for pre-launch pilot.
- Partnerships:
  - Ecommerce agencies, Shopify experts, and fashion consultants.

### Track A (Growth Stores) Recommended Channels
- Cold outbound email with pilot-first CTA.
- Founder-led social proof posts.
- Fast demo and short onboarding calls.

### Track B (Enterprise) Recommended Channels
- Warm intros, partner referrals, and executive outreach.
- Custom ROI narrative and security/procurement readiness.
- Longer sales cycle with multi-stakeholder alignment.

## 6) 30/60/90 Plan
### Days 1-30
- Publish 12 short videos.
- Outreach to first 150 qualified brands.
- Book 20 pilot calls.
- Activate 10 trial accounts.

### Days 31-60
- Publish customer-style pilot snapshots (with permission).
- Outreach to additional 300 brands.
- Close 15 active pilots.
- Convert 5 paying accounts.

### Days 61-90
- Build partner/referral lane with agencies.
- Run webinar: "How fashion brands launch AI try-on without breaking conversion flow."
- Convert 10 more paying accounts.

## 7) KPI Dashboard
- Top-of-funnel:
  - Outreach emails sent/week
  - Reply rate
  - Demo call booking rate
- Mid-funnel:
  - Trial activations
  - Time-to-first successful try-on
  - Pilot completion rate
- Revenue:
  - Trial-to-paid conversion
  - Average plan selected
  - Monthly recurring revenue from new cohorts

## 8) Offer Angles To Test
- "Pre-launch partner cohort" (limited slots).
- "14-day guided pilot for fashion stores."
- "Conversion-focused AI try-on rollout for upper-body catalog."
- "Agency multi-brand pilot package."

## 9) High-Intent CTA
- Primary CTA: "Book a 20-minute pilot setup call."
- Secondary CTA: "Start free trial and run first 10 products this week."

## 10) Non-Negotiables In Messaging
- Do not claim full-body support now.
- Do not promise guaranteed conversion uplift.
- Keep claims evidence-based and pilot-oriented.
